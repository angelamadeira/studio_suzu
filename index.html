The essence

Carefully crafted shapes

We don't sell the sweet — we design the shape it comes from. Every mold is authorial: conceived, sculpted and signed here, so that your dessert looks like no one else's. It is born where two crafts meet: the precision of the one who models in 3D and the affection of the one who cooks.

Precision

Exclusive 3D modeling, a perfect fit, a design that exists only here.

Affection

Made slowly and with care. To create is a gesture of tenderness.

Who makes it

Two crafts, one kitchen table

Precision and affection are not adjectives we picked. They are two people.

Studio Suzu is run by a married couple. One models in 3D — surfaces, tolerances, the exact angle at which a mold lets go. The other cooks, and knows what a shape has to promise before anyone tastes it. The mold is where the two meet: it exists only because neither of them could have made it alone.

This is the part no catalog can copy. Machines are for sale. A shared table is not.

The one who models

Brings the discipline: 3D modeling, exact fit, a shape that releases cleanly and repeats a thousand times over.

The one who cooks

Brings the reason: what a dessert has to say when it reaches the table, and how it must feel in the hand.

The logo

suzu is the Japanese bell — a small, delicate charm, exactly like what the brand makes. That is why the seal is round: the shape of the bell. The word is drawn in Jost Light, lowercase and vectorized; the ideogram is the signature, reserved for seals.

How to use it

Always

  • Use the outline seal as the official logo: graphite #3A3038 on light, white on dark.
  • Let the round seal be the hero — avatar, flavors, full backgrounds. The inline is for signatures and bands.
  • Keep clear space of at least ¼ of the seal's diameter on all four sides.
  • Respect the minimum sizes: 32 px (10 mm) outline seal, 24 px (8 mm) filled seal, 80 px (25 mm) inline.
  • Below 32 px, prefer the filled seal — the solid disc survives what the hairline ring does not.
  • Over any pattern, set the seal inside a solid clearing.
  • Keep the ring's stroke at 52/1600. It equals the Jost Light stem — seal and type, one pen.
  • Set the letters in ink #463C34 or white, and nothing else.
  • Fill the seal with a palette color only inside the flavor system.
  • Use the wordmark exactly as supplied: vectorized, upright, undistorted.
  • Keep every fill flat. No gradients, no shadows, no effects.

On light, gold, stone and wood are too pale to be letters, so they darken into the · text variants above; on charcoal the base color already reads, so it is reused. Values marked α carry an alpha channel and sit among the others, in the group they belong to. They composite over whatever is beneath them, so the hex alone is not the color — their swatch shows each one over white and over charcoal. Never flatten an α value into a solid hex: it will be wrong on every surface but one.

on dark — the accent becomes type

On light, no accent can be a letter — not one of them reaches 4.5:1, which is exactly why the deep column exists. On charcoal the accent is already the lightest thing on screen, so it stops being a fill and becomes the type. The deep column is for light only; on dark, use the base color itself.
